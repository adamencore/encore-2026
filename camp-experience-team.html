Theater & warm-up games

Quick, low-prep drama games for warm-ups and building ensemble energy. Tap a game to see how to play.

1 Emotion TelephonePass an emotion down the line and watch how it changes ▾
Materials: emotion cards or slips (excited, sad, nervous, angry, thrilled, sleepy) · a quiet space

How to play

  1. Line the players up facing the same direction, so they can't see each other — like a relay line.
  2. The first Young Artist pulls an emotion card (without showing the others) and acts it out for the person behind them — facial expression, body language, and a single sound effect (a sigh, a laugh).
  3. That person observes, then turns and passes the emotion to the next — no talking or explaining, just acting it out.
  4. The last Young Artist performs the emotion they received for everyone. Then the first reveals the original card, and the group sees how much it changed down the line.
  5. Shuffle the order and try a new emotion.

Optional twist

Add a situation for extra fun — "Excited on a roller coaster" or "Sleepy at a royal banquet."

2 Magical MirrorMirror a partner's movements and expressions as perfectly as possible ▾
Materials: just some space to spread out

How to play

  1. Pair up, facing each other. One is the Leader, one is the Mirror.
  2. The Leader makes slow, exaggerated movements; the Mirror mimics as closely as possible, like a reflection — hand gestures, facial expressions, dance moves, or a character (a graceful queen, a shy princess).
  3. Once comfortable, the Leader gradually picks up the pace while keeping movements smooth and clear to follow.
  4. Switch roles so the Mirror becomes the Leader; try new movements, characters, or emotions.
  5. Come back together and talk about what it was like to lead and to mirror.

Variation

Let pairs perform their mirroring for the whole group — a little friendly competition for who can stay most in sync.

Why it works

Builds listening, observing, and responding — all essential for acting — and lets pairs create expressive movement together in a light-hearted way.

3 Character SwitcherooJump between characters and emotions on the spot ▾
Materials: character cards (pirate, queen, superhero, fairy, animal) · emotion cards (happy, grumpy, excited, scared, confused) · space to move

How to play

  1. Each Young Artist draws one character card and one emotion card, then acts out that character with that emotion, moving around the room in character.
  2. Give everyone a few moments to fully embody the combo — exaggerate expressions, movement, voice, and sound.
  3. Call out "Switcheroo!" at random. On the command, everyone freezes, finds someone near them, and trades character and emotion cards.
  4. They jump straight into the new combo — adjusting voice, posture, and expression on the spot. Keep calling "Switcheroo!" every few minutes.
  5. After a few rounds, gather to share favorite character-emotion combos.

Variation

Add location cards for even more context — "in a spooky cave" or "at a royal ball."

Why it works

Keeps everyone on their toes, sparks creativity with each new combination, and builds the skill of staying in character while quickly adapting.
